Key Findings06
Data-Backed Observations

The dYdX paradox: best IR, worst price

dYdX scores 79/100 with best-in-class transparency: annual ecosystem reports, analyst calls with institutional guests, named CEO, 100% trading fees to stakers, 75% net revenue buyback. The token is down 91% from ATH. Transparency alone does not drive price. The question for regulators: does mandatory disclosure create a different kind of market, one that is more institutional, longer-duration, and less speculative, even if price doesn't respond?

79/100 IR Score · 91% ATH drawdown · $78M FDV

Hyperliquid's intentional opacity

Widest pillar dispersion in the benchmark: 53 points between Narrative (18/20) and Communication (7/20). Hyperliquid generated $844M in 2025 revenue and executed $644M in buybacks, accounting for 46% of all crypto buybacks. 11-person team, no VC, intentionally minimal communications. If some tokens sustain massive market caps without structured disclosure, what does that mean for mandatory frameworks?

65/100 IR Score · $8.67B FDV · $844M 2025 revenue

Strong fundamentals, no IR: the Kamino and Morpho gap

Kamino dominates Solana lending with $2.4B TVL (60%+ market share) and $34.8M cumulative revenue, yet scores 52/100. Zero formal reporting cadence, no token holder calls, no treasury dashboard. Morpho has $3.8B TVL, Apollo acquiring 9% of supply, and Coinbase building $960M+ in loans on top, yet $0 of protocol revenue flows to token holders. Two of DeFi's fastest-growing protocols have massive gaps between what they build and what they disclose. These are the projects that institutional capital wants to own but can't underwrite.

Key Findings (cont.)07

Voluntary frameworks exist, adoption is low

The Blockworks Token Transparency Framework (TTF), launched in June 2025 with backing from Pantera, L1D, and Theia, was presented directly to the SEC. It covers 18 criteria including supply allocation, market-making arrangements, financial disclosures, and team verification. Of our benchmark universe, only 5 of 20 protocols (25%) have filed. Beyond the TTF: 0% disclose market maker terms, 6% have a dedicated IR hub, 44% disclose circulating float. The infrastructure for standardized token disclosure is being built. The question is whether adoption remains voluntary or becomes a regulatory baseline.

25% TTF filed · 0% MM terms · Presented to SEC June 2025

Six distinct value accrual models

The benchmark captures at least six mechanisms: direct fee distribution (dYdX, GMX), buyback-and-burn (Meteora, Orca), conditional buybacks (Lido NEST), ve-model epoch distributions (Aerodrome), governance-only with no fee switch (Compound, Morpho), and staking revenue sharing (Pendle). Regulators considering token classification should understand that "token" is not a monolith. The ownership, accrual, and alignment structures vary as much as equity structures in traditional markets.

Methodology08
Five-Pillar Framework · v2.4

Data Access (20) — First-party data availability plus coverage across five mandatory third-party platforms: Artemis, Token Terminal, Dune Analytics, Nansen, Blockworks Research.

Communication (20) — Investor-facing communication cadence: quarterly updates, token holder calls, dedicated IR channel, investor deck.

Transparency (20) — Structural transparency: float disclosure, revenue segmentation, market maker terms, exchange listing disclosure.

Narrative (20) — Quality and coherence of market positioning relative to fundamentals.

Value Accrual (20) — Token holder value return mechanisms: buyback programs, fee distribution, staking yield, burn mechanics.
