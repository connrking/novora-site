Voluntary Frameworks: Built but Unadopted03
The Blockworks Token Transparency Framework (TTF) — presented to SEC June 2025
25% adoption rate

The Blockworks TTF covers 18 criteria across supply, allocation, financials, and market structure. Backed by Pantera, L1D, and Theia and presented directly to the SEC. Of 53 protocols audited, 13 have filed: Jito, Jupiter, Raydium, Morpho, Aerodrome, MetaDAO, Maple, dYdX, Euler, Marinade, EtherFi, Gains Network, and Meteora. Adoption is growing but still voluntary, and three-quarters of protocols have not opted in.

0% disclose market maker terms

Across 53 protocols generating billions in combined daily volume, not a single one publicly discloses the terms of its market-making arrangements. Market maker agreements directly affect liquidity, price discovery, and the risk profile of every token holder. In traditional markets, this information is regulated. In crypto, it is invisible.

2% have an IR hub

Only Meteora (ir.meteora.ag) operates a dedicated investor relations hub that consolidates token holder reports, segmented revenue, per-token metrics, and investor materials in a single location. Every other protocol in this audit distributes information across blogs, governance forums, and third-party platforms with no centralized investor-facing experience.

Six distinct mechanisms

Direct fee distribution (dYdX, GMX). Buyback-and-burn (Meteora, Jito, Hyperliquid). Conditional buybacks (Lido NEST). Ve-model epoch distributions (Aerodrome, Curve). Staking revenue sharing (Pendle). Burn-mint equilibrium (Render). Governance-only tokens with no economic rights include some of the largest in DeFi: Uniswap, Compound, Kamino, Morpho, Optimism, Polygon. A deeper analysis of how these models perform is the subject of the companion report.

Coverage is distribution

Token Terminal, Dune, Artemis, and DefiLlama each cover 92–98% of the dataset. The bottleneck is Blockworks Research at 32% — the only platform with selective, curated coverage. Every protocol in this audit is listed on at least one platform. The data infrastructure for institutional analysis is largely built. The curation and interpretation layer is not.

Key Findings06

98% have the data. 2% package it for investors.

Almost every protocol makes revenue data publicly accessible through on-chain analytics or third-party platforms. Only Meteora has built a dedicated IR hub that aggregates this into an investor-facing experience. Protocols are not hiding their fundamentals. They are failing to present them.

98% revenue data · 2% IR hub · 49% revenue segmented

The dYdX paradox: best IR, worst price

dYdX scores 79/100 with annual ecosystem reports, analyst calls, named CEO, 100% trading fees to stakers, 75% net revenue buyback. The token is down 91% from ATH. Transparency alone does not drive price. The case for mandatory disclosure rests on market structure: informed capital, lower asymmetry, reduced fraud risk.

79/100 IR Score · 91% ATH drawdown · $78M FDV

Strong fundamentals, no IR: the Kamino and Morpho gap

Kamino dominates Solana lending with $2.4B TVL and $34.8M cumulative revenue, yet scores 52/100. Morpho has $3.8B TVL and Apollo acquiring 9% of supply, yet $0 flows to token holders. The projects institutional capital wants to own but can't underwrite.

Kamino: 52/100, $2.4B TVL · Morpho: 71/100, $0 holder revenue

Hyperliquid's intentional opacity

Widest pillar dispersion in the benchmark: Narrative 18/20, Communication 7/20. $844M in 2025 revenue and $644M in buybacks, accounting for 46% of all crypto buybacks. 11-person team, no VC, intentionally minimal communications. Methodology08
Transparency Audit Framework · 53 Protocols · 15 Metrics

This report audits 53 crypto protocols across 15 binary, publicly verifiable disclosure metrics. Each metric is assessed as true or false based on publicly observable information at time of assessment (April 2026). Artemis coverage was verified via API. Token Terminal, Dune Analytics, and DefiLlama coverage was verified via public project page searches. Blockworks Research coverage was assessed from prior IR analyses.

Disclosure metrics (10): IR Hub, Revenue Data, Revenue Segmented, Float Disclosed, Quarterly Updates, Token Holder Report, MM Terms Disclosed, Exchange Listing Disclosed, TTF Filed, Active Value Accrual.

Third-party coverage (5): Artemis, Token Terminal, Dune Analytics, DefiLlama, Blockworks Research. Each platform is checked for whether the protocol is listed with verifiable data.

Active Value Accrual is defined as the protocol having at least one live mechanism directing economic value to token holders beyond governance rights. This includes fee distribution, buyback/burn, staking yield, ve-model distributions, and burn-mint equilibrium.

Scope: Protocols were selected from the Novora IR Benchmark Database (n=53), which includes protocols with full five-pillar IR Scores (n=33) and protocols audited on transparency metrics only (n=20). All protocols have live tokens and verifiable on-chain activity. Pre-TGE protocols were excluded.

Novora IR Score evaluates protocol investor relations quality across five equally-weighted pillars (each /20, composite /100): Data Access, Communication, Transparency, Narrative, Value Accrual. Scoring methodology v2.4. Powered by Artemis.

All metrics reflect publicly available information at time of assessment. This report measures disclosure quality. It is not investment advice.
